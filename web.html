Week

In a day

Afternoon to evening (12 - 18 o'clock) send the most people;

Within a year

July (summer) and the third quarter (july - september) are peak release seasons

Weekday distribution

Looking at the number of Weekday releases, you may find that the number of midweek (Tuesday - thursday) releases is relatively stable, while the number of Friday and Saturday releases is up, and the number of Sunday releases may be down slightly.

Hour distribution

At different hours of the day, the number of posts shows a clear periodicity. The number of posts usually reaches a peak between 6 pm and 10 pm, which is consistent with people's daily schedule. Evening is the main time period for people to relax and entertainment, and people pay more attention to sports videos. In the early morning, the number of posts is very small.

Month distribution

In the dimension of Month, the number of releases in some months is larger, which may be related to the arrangement of sports events and seasonal characteristics in that Month.

Quarter Distribution

On a quarterly basis, you may find a relatively high number of releases in Q2 and Q4.

The analysis of tags reveals a high concentration of core keywords such as "sports," "football," and "league," which dominate approximately one-third of the tags, reflecting the prevalent interests among viewers. The presence of tags like "shorts," "highlight," and "viral" indicates that short-form content and highlight reels are more likely to be shared, suggesting a preference for easily digestible content. Additionally, UMAP clustering demonstrates clear semantic groupings, with distinct clusters indicating significant differences in meaning between them. Notably, certain clusters, such as Cluster 1 and 3, show superior performance in terms of views and engagement, pointing towards effective content focus areas.


The examination of video descriptions uncovers a broad range of keywords, as depicted in the word cloud, which includes types of sports, event names, and trending tags, mirroring the content themes and global reach. The length of descriptions tends to increase with video duration, with shorter videos featuring more concise descriptions and longer ones providing more detailed information. Cluster analysis of descriptions also highlights stylistic differences in content performance and semantic styles. Overwhelmingly, more than 95% of descriptions exhibit a positive sentiment, which is beneficial for attracting user clicks and interactions.

The analysis of video titles, guided by LDA, indicates a focus on themes like "matches," "players," and "highlights," suggesting that titles are crafted to capture the essence of the content. High-frequency words in the word cloud are often associated with compelling events or figures, indicating that titles are designed to attract viewers. There is a correlation between title length and video duration, with shorter videos typically having briefer titles, accommodating viewer reading habits and platform recommendation algorithms. Duration analysis shows that short videos, especially those under 3 minutes, have a significantly higher interaction rate compared to longer content, suggesting an advantage for short-form content. The 8-12 minute range offers a balanced approach, maintaining information density while retaining viewer attention. Furthermore, short videos exhibit higher engagement efficiency, with better like-to-view and comment-to-view ratios, reflecting content preferences in the era of fragmented attention spans.
